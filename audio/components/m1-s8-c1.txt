If you only remember one sentence from module one, make it this one. Every standard yes-no question about a DFA or CFG — acceptance, emptiness, equivalence for DFAs, membership and emptiness for CFGs — has a decision algorithm, because each one reduces to a finite search. The Turing machine just has to enumerate finitely many candidates and check each. Module two will introduce the first questions where that finite-search story breaks down — and once it breaks down, undecidability is everywhere.
