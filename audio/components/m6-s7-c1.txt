In simple terms, PSPACE, which stands for Polynomial Space, is a complexity class that is equal to NPSPACE, as shown by Savitch's theorem. This means that any problem solvable by a non-deterministic machine using polynomial space can also be solved by a deterministic machine using polynomial space. In this class, we find problems like TQBF, which asks whether a certain logical formula is true for all variable assignments, and Generalized Geography, a strategic game. These problems are considered PSPACE-complete, meaning they are among the hardest in PSPACE. Essentially, PSPACE encompasses decision problems where the solutions resemble perfect strategies in games that can unfold over polynomially many moves. The key takeaway is that PSPACE captures the complexity of problems solvable within a limited amount of memory.
