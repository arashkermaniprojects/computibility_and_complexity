If you’re looking to dive deeper into log-space machines and palindromes, a great place to start is with Sipser’s textbook. In section 8.4, you’ll find clear definitions of L and NL, along with some insightful examples that really illustrate the concepts. It’s a foundational resource that many students find helpful.

Also, check out the lecture notes from your course. Module 7 has some key information that can reinforce what you’re learning. It’s often useful to see concepts presented in different ways, so these notes can complement what you read in Sipser.

Don’t forget about the companion problems, too. They’re a fantastic way to apply what you’ve learned. Problems 42 and 43 discuss NL-PATH and show that PATH is NL-complete, which is crucial for understanding the boundaries of log-space. And for a real challenge, problem 44 involves inductive counting, which can deepen your grasp of these concepts. 

Engaging with these resources will give you a well-rounded understanding, so take your time with them!
