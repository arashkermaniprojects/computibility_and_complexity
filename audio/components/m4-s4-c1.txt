This is one of the most important theorems for understanding why the class P is well-defined. Sipser, theorem seven point eight: any multi-tape Turing machine running in time t of n can be simulated by a single-tape Turing machine in time order t of n squared. The square is the cost of paying for the extra tape simulation. The consequence is the part you should remember: polynomial time on a multi-tape machine is still polynomial time on a single-tape machine, because the square of a polynomial is a polynomial. That is exactly the robustness property that makes P a meaningful class — it does not change when you swap the model.
