Lock these two words down before you go further — they get conflated constantly. Recognizable, also called Turing-recognizable or r-e, means there is a Turing machine that accepts every string in the language. On strings outside the language it might reject, or it might loop forever. The machine is not obliged to halt. Decidable, also called recursive, means there is a Turing machine that always halts and gives the right yes-no answer. Decidable is strictly stronger. Every decidable language is recognizable, but the next slide shows A sub TM is recognizable and not decidable — the gap is real.
