Closure properties · Decidable vs Recognizable

Decidable is closed under everything. Recognizable is NOT closed under complement — and that gap is the bridge theorem.

1 · Theory
Decidable closures

Decidable is closed under ∪, ∩, complement, concatenation, star. Easy: build a decider that runs the constituent deciders and combines results.

Recognizable closures

Recognizable is closed under ∪, ∩, concatenation, star — but NOT complement. ATM is recognizable; ¬ATM is not.

Bridge theorem

L is decidable ⇔ both L and L̅ are recognizable.

3 · Q&A defence notes
  • Q1. Why is recognizable not closed under complement?
    A recognizer for L may loop on x ∉ L. To recognize ¬L, we'd want to accept x ∉ L, which means detecting that the original recognizer loops — equivalent to halting problem.
  • Q2. Why is recognizable closed under union but the recognizer doesn't just "run both"?
    It does: simulate both in parallel (interleaved), accept if either accepts. Parallel simulation is the standard trick.
  • Q3. Prove the bridge theorem.
    (⇒) Decidable ⇒ recognizable; ¬L also decidable ⇒ recognizable. (⇐) Both L and ¬L recognizable. Simulate both in parallel on any input x; exactly one accepts (it's in L or ¬L). Halt.
