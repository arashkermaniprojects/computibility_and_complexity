CLIQUE → VERTEX-COVER via complement

A k-clique in G ⇔ a (n−k)-vertex-cover in G̅.

CLO-4R2·30 ptsL11
1 · Theory
Lemma

Let G be a graph on n vertices. S is a clique of size k in G ⇔ V \ S is a vertex cover of size n − k in G̅ (the complement graph).

Reduction (G, k) ↦ (G̅, n − k) is polynomial and preserves the answer. Hence VERTEX-COVER is NP-hard.

3 · Q&A defence notes
  • Q1. Why does the complement work?
    Edges of G connect clique-members (forming the clique). Edges of G̅ are everything else — and a vertex cover of those is precisely the complement of the clique.
  • Q2. How long does the reduction take?
    Computing G̅ is O(n²). Computing n − k is O(1). Polynomial.
  • Q3. Is this also a reduction from VC to CLIQUE?
    Yes, by symmetry: VC(G, m) ⇔ CLIQUE(G̅, n − m). The two problems are equivalent up to complement.
