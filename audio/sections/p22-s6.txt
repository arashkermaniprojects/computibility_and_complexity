If you want to dive deeper into the concepts we’ve discussed about SAT and NP-completeness, a great resource is Sipser’s book. Check out Theorem 7.37, which covers the Cook–Levin theorem. It’s a cornerstone in computational theory and really helps clarify the relationship between problems in NP and how they can relate to SAT.

For more visual learners, the lecture notes are also really helpful. You can look at Module 5, specifically slides 3 and 4. Slide 3 presents the Cook–Levin tableau, which is an excellent way to visualize how we can represent computations. Slide 4 goes into the NP-complete chain, showing how different NP-complete problems are interrelated.

And don’t forget to tackle the companion problems. Problem 21 is great for understanding the difference between a verifier and a solver. Problem 26 helps you explore the transformation from SAT to 3SAT, which is really crucial in understanding reductions. Finally, Problem 55 focuses specifically on the Cook–Levin theorem, giving you a chance to engage with the material in a hands-on way. So, grab those resources and start exploring!
