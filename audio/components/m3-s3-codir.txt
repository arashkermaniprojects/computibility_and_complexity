Quick reminder on the co-notation. The bar over a language name, or the prefix "co-", denotes the complement — every string NOT in the language. So co-A-sub-T-M means the set of pairs ⟨M, w⟩ where M does NOT accept w. We introduced this in Module 2, slide 5, as a canonical example of a co-recognizable but undecidable language. Writing the reduction as A-sub-T-M into the complement of E-sub-L-B-A means "given ⟨M, w⟩ we build an L-B-A whose language is empty if and only if M accepts w." That's equivalent to writing co-A-sub-T-M into E-sub-L-B-A. Either form proves E-sub-L-B-A undecidable, because decidability is closed under complement.
