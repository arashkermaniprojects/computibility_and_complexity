Immerman–Szelepcsényi · NL = coNL

Count reachable vertices inductively; conclude ¬PATH ∈ NL.

CLO-5R3·40 ptsL15
1 · Theory
Theorem · NL = coNL

For each i, let ci = |{v : reachable from s in ≤ i steps}|. Compute c0, c1, …, cn inductively. Using cn as a witness count, we can certify "t is unreachable" by witnessing all cn reachable vertices and showing t isn't one of them.

Surprising result: nondeterministic space is closed under complement (unlike nondeterministic time, where NP = coNP is open).

3 · Q&A defence notes
  • Q1. Why does counting help?
    Once we know cn, we can verify "v is unreachable" by witnessing all cn reachable vertices ≠ v. The witness is implicit (guesses) but counted explicitly.
  • Q2. Why does this work for NL but not for NP?
    In NL, configurations are polynomial; counting them and re-verifying fits in log space. In NP, the time budget is polynomial but witnesses are exponentially many.
  • Q3. What does NL = coNL really mean?
    PATH and ¬PATH are both in NL. So "is there a path?" and "is there no path?" have the same nondeterministic space complexity. This makes NL "self-dual" — unlike NP, presumably.
