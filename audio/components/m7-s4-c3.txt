In this slide, we’re discussing the hierarchy theorems, specifically focusing on the concept of diagonalization. Imagine we have a list of Turing machines, each using space that is less than or equal to a function f of the input size. Our diagonalizer, denoted as D, takes each machine M_i and operates on its own description, flipping the result to ensure that D produces an output different from M_i.

Because D requires slightly more space than f, it cannot be confined within the same space bounds, meaning that the class of languages recognizable in space g is strictly larger than those recognizable in space f. This illustrates a key point: as we increase our space limits, we can recognize strictly more languages. 

The takeaway here is that the hierarchy theorems show how increasing computational resources, like space, expands our ability to solve problems.
