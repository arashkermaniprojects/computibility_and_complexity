In this course, we explored the limits of what can be computed. Algorithms are constrained by two main factors: what’s computable and the resources they use. Diagonalization shows that some problems are undecidable, meaning no algorithm can solve them. On the other hand, the time and space hierarchy theorems help us understand the different classes of problems that can be solved within the realm of the decidable. We’ve seen a few proven separations between these classes, but many questions remain unanswered, with P versus NP being the most famous. This ongoing exploration shapes the field of modern complexity theory. The key takeaway is that understanding these boundaries helps us navigate the intricate landscape of computation and problem-solving.
