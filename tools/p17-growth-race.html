3 · Q&A defence notes
  • Q1. Why use a log scale?
    Because 2ⁿ at n=40 is ~10¹², while n² is 1600. Linear scale would crush all polynomials to a single line at the bottom.
  • Q2. Where does 2ⁿ overtake n³?
    Around n = 10. After that 2ⁿ grows roughly 2× per step; n³ only grows like (n+1)³/n³ ≈ 1 + 3/n.
  • Q3. Why is polynomial vs exponential the "right" boundary?
    Polynomials are closed under composition; exponentials are not. Robust across reasonable machine models (Cobham-Edmonds thesis).
