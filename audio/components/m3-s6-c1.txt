Rice's theorem says every interesting question about what a Turing machine's language looks like — is it empty, is it regular, is it finite — is undecidable. This diagram lays out the proof in three panels.

Panel one. The witness machines. Suppose for contradiction that some property P is nontrivial and semantic, and some Turing machine R can decide P. Because P is nontrivial, two specific Turing machines must exist out there in the universe. The first one, T-yes, has a language that DOES have property P. The second one, T-no, has a language that does NOT have property P. We pick T-no to be the simplest one possible: the trivial machine that rejects every input, so its language is the empty set. These two witnesses get FIXED once and for all. They are not constructed from the input; they are just out there, and we know they exist because P is nontrivial.

Think of T-yes and T-no as the two BEHAVIOURS our reduction will be choosing between.

Panel two. The construction. Given any pair of a Turing machine M and a string w, we build a brand new Turing machine, call it M-sub-M-w. Here's what M-sub-M-w does on any input x. First, it ignores x for a moment and runs M on w. This is the gate. If M does not accept w, M-sub-M-w just rejects x and stops. If M does accept w, M-sub-M-w hands x over to T-yes and copies T-yes's answer.

Look at the two branches. When M accepts w, M-sub-M-w behaves EXACTLY like T-yes — it accepts the same strings, so its language equals L of T-yes, which by definition has property P. When M does NOT accept w, M-sub-M-w rejects everything — its language is empty, identical to L of T-no, which does NOT have P. So the question "does L of M-sub-M-w have property P?" is exactly the question "does M accept w?".

Panel three. The contradiction. Build a decider for A-sub-T-M as follows: given any pair M and w, construct the description of M-sub-M-w, hand it to R, and copy R's answer. By the construction we just verified, R's answer for M-sub-M-w is "yes" exactly when M accepts w. So we've decided A-sub-T-M. But A-sub-T-M is undecidable, from Module 2. Contradiction. R cannot exist. Property P is undecidable.

The two roles in one sentence: T-yes is the good behaviour we ADOPT when M accepts w, and T-no is the bad behaviour we FALL BACK to when M does not.
