Single-tape TM on {0ⁿ1ⁿ} · O(n²) by pairing

Watch a single-tape TM shuttle back and forth; step counter grows quadratically.

CLO-3R2·30 ptsL9
1 · Theory

{0n1n} requires equal counts. With ONE tape and ONE head, the only strategy is: pair off 0s with 1s by marking. Each pair costs an O(n) round-trip. n/2 pairs ⇒ O(n²) steps total.

Claim

Single-tape TM deciding {0n1n} requires Θ(n²) time. Two-tape: O(n).

3 · Q&A defence notes
  • Q1. Why exactly O(n²)?
    n/2 pairs × O(n) per pair = O(n²). The O(n) per pair is the head's round-trip across the input.
  • Q2. Can a single-tape TM do better than O(n²)?
    No — proven by a crossing-sequence argument. Two-tape, two-head, etc. can do O(n).
  • Q3. Why doesn't this break P?
    O(n²) is still polynomial. P is robust across reasonable machine models — multi-tape ↔ single-tape costs at most quadratic blowup.
