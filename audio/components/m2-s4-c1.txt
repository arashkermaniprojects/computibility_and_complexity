This is your first reduction, and it sets the template for everything that follows. The goal is to show HALT sub TM is undecidable. We argue by contradiction: assume some Turing machine R decides HALT sub TM. We then build a decider S for A sub TM out of R. The construction goes like this. On input angle-M, w, the decider S first asks R whether M halts on w. If R says no, S rejects — because if M does not halt, M certainly does not accept. If R says yes, S simulates M on w directly, knowing it will halt, and outputs whatever M outputs. S decides A sub TM. But A sub TM is undecidable — contradiction. So R cannot exist, and HALT sub TM is undecidable. The whole proof is one paragraph once you see the pattern.
