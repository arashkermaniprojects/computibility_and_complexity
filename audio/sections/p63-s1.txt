Let’s dive into the theorem that states NP is a subset of EXPTIME. What this means is that every language in NP can be decided by a deterministic Turing machine, or DTM, in a specific amount of time. More precisely, if you have a nondeterministic Turing machine, or NTM, that halts on every branch in time n raised to the k power, the DTM can simulate it in time that’s exponential, specifically 2 raised to the order of n to the k.

Now, why is this important? It gives us the first solid connection in the hierarchy of complexity classes: P, NP, and EXPTIME. When we combine this finding with the time hierarchy theorem, which tells us that P is strictly contained within EXPTIME, we reach a fascinating conclusion. At least one of these statements must be true: either P is not equal to NP, or NP is not equal to EXPTIME. However, we don’t yet know which one it is. This opens up a lot of questions and possibilities in computational theory, making it a fundamental piece of the puzzle in understanding complexity classes.
