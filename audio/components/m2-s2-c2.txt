The widget below is a universal Turing machine. The transitions you write in the box are the program — they describe some specific machine M. The universal TM, call it U, reads those transitions and interprets them step by step, exactly like a programming-language interpreter. This is the deep idea Turing introduced in nineteen thirty-six: that a single fixed machine can run any program. Try the three presets. The most important one is loops forever — load it, hit run, and the simulator caps at two hundred steps with no halt. That is precisely the behavior that makes A sub TM recognizable but not decidable: if M does not halt on w, U does not halt either, and there is no way to tell from outside whether you are still mid-computation or stuck forever.
