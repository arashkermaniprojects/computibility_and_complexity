Time hierarchy theorem · TIME(f) ⊊ TIME(f log f)

Diagonalize against time-bounded TMs.

Theorem (Hartmanis & Stearns, 1965)

If f(n) ≥ n log n is time-constructible, then TIME(f) ⊊ TIME(f · log f). The log overhead is the cost of simulation.

Implication: TIME(n) ⊊ TIME(n log² n), so P contains strictly more languages than linear-time. Direct consequence: P ⊊ EXPTIME.

3 · Q&A defence notes
  • Q1. Why log f overhead?
    A universal TM simulating a TIME(f) machine needs log f extra time for bookkeeping (tape head moves, state tracking).
  • Q2. Why "time-constructible"?
    D must compute f(n) on its own clock to know when to stop. Time-constructibility says f(n) can be computed in O(f(n)) time.
  • Q3. Does this prove P ⊊ NP?
    No — P and NP differ in determinism, not in resource amount. Time hierarchy compares time bounds, not (deterministic vs nondeterministic).
