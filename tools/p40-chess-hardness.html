Generalized chess on an n×n board is PSPACE-hard

Encoding TQBF into chess positions on a polynomial-size board.

CLO-4R3·40 ptsL14
1 · Theory

"Generalized chess" allows n×n boards with appropriately scaled piece counts. The problem GAME-CHESS asks: from a given position, does White have a winning strategy?

Theorem (Fraenkel–Lichtenstein, 1981)

Generalized chess (without the 50-move rule) is EXPTIME-complete. Adding a polynomial move bound makes it PSPACE-complete.

3 · Q&A defence notes
  • Q1. Why does standard 8×8 chess have a P-time solution?
    Finite board + finite positions = bounded game tree. Brute-force minimax in constant time (depending on a huge constant). The PSPACE-hardness story requires the board size to grow.
  • Q2. What changes between PSPACE and EXPTIME?
    Without a move bound, games can last exponentially many moves ⇒ EXPTIME. With a polynomial move bound, PSPACE.
  • Q3. What else is EXPTIME-complete?
    Generalised Go (without ko), Shogi, many other complex games on n×n boards.
