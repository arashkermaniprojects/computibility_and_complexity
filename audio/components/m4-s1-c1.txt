This is the single most important picture in the whole module. Polynomial growth, the left side of the race, is what we call feasible — and it is exactly what the class P captures. Exponential growth, on the right, is not. The gap is not subtle. Look at n equals forty: between n squared and two to the n, you already have roughly a trillion-fold difference. That is the difference between a problem your laptop solves before you finish reading the question, and a problem the heat death of the universe runs out of steps for.
