If you want to dive deeper into the concepts of verifiers and brute-force solvers, a great starting point is Michael Sipser's book. Check out section 7.3 for a solid definition of NP. It breaks down what it means for a problem to be in NP and really clarifies the role of verifiers. Then, move on to section 7.4, which covers NP-completeness. This part helps you understand why certain problems are tougher than others and what it means for a problem to be NP-complete.

Also, take a look at some lecture notes. There’s a helpful diagram in Module 5 that illustrates the equivalence between verifiers and non-deterministic Turing machines. It’s a visual way to grasp how these concepts connect.

Finally, don’t miss the companion problems. Problem 22 looks at SAT in an interactive way, while Problem 23 focuses on the HAMPATH verifier. Problem 24 showcases the P class, which will help you see the contrast with NP. These exercises can really solidify your understanding.
