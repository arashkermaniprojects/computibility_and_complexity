3COLOR is NP-complete · 3SAT → 3COLOR

Variable gadgets + OR-gadgets. Graph is 3-colorable iff formula is satisfiable.

CLO-4R2·30 ptsL12
1 · Theory
Gadget pieces

3 base colors: T (true), F (false), B (base reference). Variable gadget: triangle (x, ¬x, B) — forces x and ¬x to take T and F. OR-gadget for clause (a ∨ b ∨ c): a specific 5-node graph forcing at least one of a, b, c to be T.

Polynomial-size graph; 3-colorable iff formula satisfiable.

3 · Q&A defence notes
  • Q1. Why a triangle for variable gadget?
    Three mutually-connected nodes need three different colors. With one fixed to B (base), the other two are forced to be {T, F} — encoding x and ¬x.
  • Q2. What's the OR-gadget structure?
    A small 5-node graph with inputs a, b, c connected such that the graph is 3-colorable iff at least one of a, b, c is colored T.
  • Q3. Polynomial?
    n variable gadgets (constant size) + m OR-gadgets (constant size each) = O(n+m) nodes. Edges: O(n+m). Polynomial.
