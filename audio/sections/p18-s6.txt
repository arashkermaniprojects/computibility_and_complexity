If you’re looking to dive deeper into single-tape Turing machines and their complexities, there are some excellent resources available. You can start with Sipser’s book, specifically Example 7.2, which explores the pair-off algorithm. This is a great way to visualize how the Turing machine processes pairs of symbols and helps to solidify your understanding of how it operates.

Also, check out Theorem 7.8 in the same book. It discusses how multi-tape Turing machines can simulate single-tape ones, which is crucial for grasping the differences in computational power and efficiency between these models. 

For more practical applications, I recommend looking at the lecture notes from Module 4, particularly the sections that explain the back-and-forth movement of the tape head. 

Lastly, don’t forget about the companion problem, P19, where you get to explore a race between two tapes. This problem encourages you to think critically about the performance characteristics of different Turing machine configurations. These resources will deepen your understanding and enhance your skills in analyzing computational models.
