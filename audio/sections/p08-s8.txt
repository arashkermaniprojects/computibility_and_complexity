If you want to dive deeper into mapping reductions and undecidability, a great place to start is with Michael Sipser's textbook. Check out Theorem 5.4 and the section on mapping reductions in Chapter 5. Sipser lays out the concepts in a clear way, which can really help solidify your understanding.

You can also look at lecture notes from your course, especially the notes on Module 2. Those cover reductions to the Halting problem, which is a classic example. And don’t miss Module 3; it includes a general reduction template that can be super helpful for understanding how to set up your own reductions.

If you want to test your understanding, tackle some companion problems. Problem 5 focuses on the Halting problem, while Problem 6 is about emptiness for Turing machines. Lastly, check out Problem 14, which deals with Rice's theorem. It’s a powerful result that generalizes these ideas to any non-trivial property of languages. These resources will give you a solid grasp of the material and help you see how these concepts fit together.
