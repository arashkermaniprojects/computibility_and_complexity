Same situation as slide 3, with the direction reversed in the obvious way. The figure footer literally states the reduction as A-sub-T-M into co-ALL-sub-C-F-G. Read off the punchline: the language of G-sub-M,w equals all of Sigma-star if and only if M does NOT accept w. So our constructed CFG belongs to ALL-sub-C-F-G if and only if M does not accept w, which means it belongs to co-ALL-sub-C-F-G if and only if M accepts w. The mapping from M and w to our CFG is therefore a reduction from A-sub-T-M into co-ALL-sub-C-F-G, not into ALL-sub-C-F-G directly.

Why does this still prove ALL-sub-C-F-G is undecidable? Same reason as before: decidability is closed under complement. Co-ALL-sub-C-F-G being undecidable is mathematically identical to ALL-sub-C-F-G being undecidable. The two statements are interchangeable. The reduction A-sub-T-M to co-ALL-sub-C-F-G is logically the same as the complement of A-sub-T-M to ALL-sub-C-F-G — bar both sides.
