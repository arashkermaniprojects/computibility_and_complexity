Let’s dive into the concept of FINITE TM, which is the set of all Turing machines where the language they recognize is finite. To understand why FINITE TM is undecidable, we can apply Rice's Theorem. Rice's Theorem tells us that any nontrivial property of the languages recognized by Turing machines is undecidable. The property of a language being finite is nontrivial because some languages are finite while others are infinite.

But if the length of x is greater than 10, M prime runs the original machine M on some specific input w.

Now, if M accepts w, then M prime can accept an infinite number of strings, making its language infinite. Conversely, if M does not accept w, M prime only accepts strings of length 10 or less, keeping its language finite. This gives us a clear connection: M accepts w if and only if the machine M prime is not in FINITE TM. Hence, FINITE TM is undecidable.
