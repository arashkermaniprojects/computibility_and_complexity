If you’re looking to dive deeper into the time hierarchy theorem, a great starting point is Michael Sipser’s textbook. Specifically, take a look at Theorem 9.10, where he lays out the details in a very clear way. The explanations and examples he provides can help solidify your understanding of how time complexity relates to different classes of problems.

In addition to the textbook, consider checking out lecture notes on the topic. There’s also a module that focuses on this theorem, and it includes some helpful visual aids and examples that can enhance your grasp of the concepts.

Finally, tackling some companion problems is a fantastic way to reinforce what you’ve learned. Problem 45 challenges you to think about the hierarchy through diagonalization, while Problem 48 explores the relationship between P and exponential time classes, which can really deepen your insight into these complexity classes. Engaging with these resources will give you a more rounded understanding of the time hierarchy theorem and its implications in computational theory.
