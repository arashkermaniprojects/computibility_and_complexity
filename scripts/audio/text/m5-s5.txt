Let us zoom out. The full map so far: P is inside N P, which is inside P space, which is inside exponential time. We know strict separations — P inside exponential time, and P inside exponential space — by hierarchy theorems we will see later. We do not know whether P equals N P. We do not know whether N P equals P space. We do not know whether P space equals exponential time. These three open questions are arguably the most important problems in theoretical computer science. The Clay Millennium Prize offers a million dollars for the first one. N P complete problems sit at the top of N P, hardest to break out of. If even one N P complete problem were in P, all of N P would collapse into P. So far no such breakthrough — despite hundreds of N P complete problems and decades of intense effort, the boundary between P and N P remains intact.
