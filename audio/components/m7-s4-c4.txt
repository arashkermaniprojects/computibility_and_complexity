In this section, we focus on some concrete separations established by the hierarchy theorems. First, we know that Nondeterministic Logarithmic space, or NL, is strictly contained within PSPACE, which is a consequence of the space hierarchy theorem. Next, we have the separation of P from EXPTIME, indicating that problems solvable in polynomial time are distinct from those solvable in exponential time. Additionally, NP is strictly contained within NEXPTIME, showing that nondeterministic polynomial time problems are also separated from their exponential counterparts. While the relationship between P and NP remains an open question, these established separations confirm that not all complexity classes are created equal. The key takeaway is that the hierarchy theorems provide us with crucial insights into the landscape of computational complexity.
