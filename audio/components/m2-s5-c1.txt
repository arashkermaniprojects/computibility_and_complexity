The bridge theorem is the single most useful tool for placing a language on this map. It says: L is decidable if and only if both L and its complement L-bar are Turing-recognizable. The forward direction is easy — decidable means there is a halting machine, and you can run the same machine and just flip the answer to recognize the complement. The reverse direction is the interesting one. Suppose you have a recognizer for L and a recognizer for L-bar. Run them in parallel, dovetailed. On any input, exactly one of them will eventually accept — and that tells you the answer. So a language lands in the "neither recognizable" region of the map exactly when at least one of L and L-bar fails to be recognizable.
