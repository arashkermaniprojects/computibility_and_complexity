TQBF ≤p Generalized Geography

Encode a QBF as a game graph. ∃-moves and ∀-moves alternate.

CLO-4R3·40 ptsL14
1 · Theory
Reduction

For each variable in the QBF, add a "choice" gadget. ∃-variables: Player 1 chooses truth value. ∀-variables: Player 2 chooses. The final propositional matrix is encoded as a path-of-no-return; Player 2 wins iff some clause is unsatisfied.

So Player 1 wins the game ⇔ ∃-player can satisfy the QBF ⇔ QBF is true.

3 · Q&A defence notes
  • Q1. Why is the move-direction tied to the quantifier?
    At ∃-gadgets, Player 1 (∃) makes a binary choice. At ∀-gadgets, Player 2 (∀) makes the choice. The "current mover" alternates with the quantifier alternation.
  • Q2. How does the propositional matrix get encoded?
    After all variables are chosen, the game reaches a "clause selection" gadget. Player 2 picks a clause; if all literals in it are false, Player 1 has no move ⇒ loses.
  • Q3. Size?
    O(n + m) nodes per variable/clause. Polynomial in formula size.
