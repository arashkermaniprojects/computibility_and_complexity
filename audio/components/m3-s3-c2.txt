In this module, we explore the reduction from a Turing machine to an LBA, or Linear Bounded Automaton. The LBA’s primary role, denoted as B with inputs M and w, is to take a candidate computation history and determine if it’s a valid accepting history for the Turing machine M on the input w. It’s important to note that B does not run M itself; instead, it verifies that the information on the tape accurately reflects a legitimate computation trace. Your task here is to categorize various assertions into two groups: what B needs to check and what falls outside its job description. Understanding this distinction is crucial as it highlights the limitations of what an LBA can do. The key takeaway is that the LBA's function is to validate traces, not to simulate the computations of a Turing machine directly.
