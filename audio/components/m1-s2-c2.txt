Sipser's theorems four point two and four point three are the immediate corollaries you get for free. The same decidability we just proved for DFAs extends to NFAs and to regular expressions, with no real new work. For an NFA: convert it to a DFA using the standard subset construction, then run the DFA decider — that conversion is mechanical, the result is still a DFA, and we already know how to decide acceptance for DFAs. For a regular expression: convert it to an NFA using Thompson's construction, then apply the previous result. The takeaway is that the four equivalent representations of regular languages — DFA, NFA, regular expression, GNFA — collapse into one decidability story.
