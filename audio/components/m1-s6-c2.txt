The whole module fits in one sentence: for DFAs and CFGs, every natural property you might want to test — acceptance, emptiness, equivalence for DFAs, and membership and emptiness for CFGs — reduces to a finite search, and finite search is exactly what a Turing machine does well. EQ sub CFG is the one exception that we will see soon: equivalence of context-free grammars is actually undecidable, which is striking because EQ sub DFA was so easy. That contrast is the bridge into module two, where the lid comes off and undecidability becomes the central topic.
