If you only remember one sentence from module two, make it this one. A diagonal argument, or a reduction from one, shows that A sub TM, HALT sub TM, E sub TM, and many other natural Turing-machine properties are undecidable. And the gap between decidable and Turing-recognizable is a real gap, not a definitional accident — there are languages that genuinely sit in one and not the other. Module three picks up from here and turns reductions into the main tool. Once you accept that A sub TM is undecidable, every other undecidability result is just bookkeeping on top of it.
