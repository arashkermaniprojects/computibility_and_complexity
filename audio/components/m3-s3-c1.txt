In this callout, we discuss how we can show that the problem of determining whether an LBA, or Linear Bounded Automaton, accepts a particular input is undecidable. We start with a Turing machine M and an input string w. The goal is to construct an LBA, which we’ll call B sub M,w. This LBA will generate the language made up of all the accepting computation histories of M when it processes w. Essentially, we are mapping the behavior of M onto the LBA. If the language of B sub M,w is not empty, it means that M accepts w. Therefore, if we could decide whether L of B sub M,w is empty, we could also decide whether M accepts w, which leads us to a contradiction. The key takeaway is that the emptiness problem for LBAs is undecidable, reflecting the limits of computation as outlined by Sipser.
