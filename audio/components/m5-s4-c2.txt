Now watch a reduction in motion. Pick a pair from the toggle row above. The first toggle, sat to three-sat, shows how a general C N F clause can be replaced by a conjunction of three-literal clauses using auxiliary variables — a long clause becomes a chain of width-three pieces. The second toggle, three-sat to clique, builds a graph with one vertex per literal-occurrence and edges between compatible literals from different clauses; a satisfying assignment becomes a k-clique. The third toggle reduces clique to vertex cover through a complement-graph trick: a k-clique in G corresponds to a vertex cover of size n minus k in the complement of G. The fourth toggle takes three-sat all the way to subset sum by encoding clause satisfaction in digit positions of carefully chosen integers. Each reduction is mechanical, polynomial in input size, and preserves yes-or-no answers. Stringing them together gives polynomial reductions between any two N P complete problems.
