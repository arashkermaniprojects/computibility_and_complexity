SAT → 3SAT · auxiliary-variable splitting

Every clause with k > 3 literals becomes a chain of 3-literal clauses.

CLO-4R2·30 ptsL11
1 · Theory
The splitting rule

(ℓ1 ∨ ℓ2 ∨ ℓ3 ∨ ℓ4) becomes (ℓ1 ∨ ℓ2 ∨ a) ∧ (¬a ∨ ℓ3 ∨ ℓ4) with a fresh variable a.

Satisfiability preserved: pick a = (ℓ3 ∨ ℓ4) effectively. Polynomial: k − 2 clauses, k − 3 auxiliary variables.

3 · Q&A defence notes
  • Q1. Why a fresh variable a?
    A new variable not appearing elsewhere lets us split without affecting other clauses. Each clause-split introduces its own a.
  • Q2. Why is satisfiability preserved?
    If original is satisfied, at least one of ℓ₁…ℓ₄ is true. Set a = whether (ℓ₃∨ℓ₄) is true. That choice satisfies both new clauses. Converse is similar.
  • Q3. What's the running time of the reduction?
    Linear in formula size. Each k-literal clause becomes k−2 clauses with k−3 new variables.
