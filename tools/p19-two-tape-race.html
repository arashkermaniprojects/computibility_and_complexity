Single-tape vs Two-tape · {0ⁿ1ⁿ} race

Same problem. Different machine model. O(n²) vs O(n).

CLO-3R2·30 ptsL9
1 · Theory

Two-tape strategy: copy the 0s to tape 2; then march tape 1 right through the 1s while tape 2 marches left through the copied 0s. Both in sync. O(n) steps.

Robustness theorem

A k-tape TM running in time t(n) can be simulated by a single-tape TM in time O(t(n)²). So polynomial stays polynomial across reasonable models.

3 · Q&A defence notes
  • Q1. Why does two-tape win so much?
    It uses tape 2 as a stack/queue, eliminating the need to shuttle the head back and forth.
  • Q2. Why doesn't this break P?
    The simulation cost is at most squared — polynomial blowup. P is defined modulo polynomial differences in machine model.
  • Q3. What's the limit of multi-tape speedup?
    Some problems get linear speedups from multi-tape; others (like sorting) require Ω(n log n) regardless of tapes.
