If you're looking to dive deeper into the concept of Quines and the recursion theorem, I'd recommend starting with Michael Sipser's textbook. In particular, check out section 6.1, which gives a solid introduction to the recursion theorem. You’ll find Theorem 6.3, which is crucial for understanding how Quines work, and then Theorem 6.5, which tells us that every Turing machine has a way to recognize its own description. 

For a more hands-on approach, look at the lecture notes from your course. There's a part in Module 3 that explains the T equals A times B construction, which is key to grasping the recursion theorem conceptually. 

After that, tackle some companion problems to really solidify your understanding. Problem 14 deals with Rice's theorem, and then you can move on to Problem 15, which covers the fixed-point version of the theorem. Finally, Problem 16 asks about self-aware Turing machines, which ties everything together nicely. Engaging with these resources will deepen your understanding and give you a well-rounded view of the topic.
