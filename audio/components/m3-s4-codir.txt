Same reminder on the co-notation for ALL-sub-C-F-G. The bar in co-A-sub-T-M means the complement of the language. We introduced this in Module 2, slide 5. In this slide the reduction is written as A-sub-T-M into the complement of ALL-sub-C-F-G — meaning, given ⟨M, w⟩ we build a context-free grammar whose language is NOT all of sigma-star if and only if M accepts w. This is equivalent to writing co-A-sub-T-M into ALL-sub-C-F-G. Either form proves ALL-sub-C-F-G undecidable, because A-sub-T-M and its complement are both undecidable.
