Big-O notation is the language complexity theory speaks in, and the picture explains why. We do not care about constants — a factor of two, a factor of a thousand, both vanish. We do not care about low-order terms — the n squared term dominates a hundred n once n is large enough. What we keep is the shape of growth: how the running time changes when the input gets twice as long. Big-O is an upper bound on that shape, taken in the limit, ignoring everything that does not survive that limit.
