If you only remember one sentence from this module, make it this one. P is the class of decision problems solvable in polynomial time on a deterministic Turing machine. It is robust across reasonable machine models, and it is the mathematical proxy for the word feasible. Everything in module five — the class NP, the Cook–Levin theorem, NP-completeness — builds on top of this definition. Knowing what P is, and being comfortable with polynomial-time reductions, is the prerequisite for the rest of the course.
