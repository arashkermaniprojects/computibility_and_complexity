1 · The Immerman–Szelepcsényi theorem · NL = coNL

Immerman 1988 and Szelepcsényi 1987 independently proved: NL = coNL. Equivalently, ¬PATH (UNREACHABILITY) is in NL. This is a surprising and deep result — nondeterministic log space is closed under complement, while nondeterministic polynomial time (NP vs coNP) is open.

Inductive counting · the technique

For each i, let ci = |{v : v is reachable from s in ≤ i steps}|. Compute c0, c1, …, cn inductively in nondeterministic log space.

Key recurrence: ci+1 = (number of vertices v such that either v is in the reach-set at step i, or v has a predecessor in the reach-set at step i). Both checks use the value ci as a witness count — the nondeterministic machine guesses which ci vertices are in the reach-set, verifies each, and counts those satisfying the property.

Using cn to decide UNREACHABILITY

Given cn = number of vertices reachable from s. To certify that t is unreachable: nondeterministically guess cn reachable vertices v1, …, vcn, each different from t, and verify each is reachable (using the PATH algorithm). If all cn are accounted for and t isn't among them, t must be unreachable.

Space used at each step: counters of size O(log n), one current vertex of size O(log n). All operations fit in log space. The whole construction takes nondeterministic log space.

3 · Pitfalls
  • The counts ci are exact, not bounded. The whole technique depends on ci being precisely the number of reachable-by-i-steps vertices. Approximation breaks the witness logic.
  • "Inductive counting" doesn't mean we store the reach-set. We only store ci. The actual reach-set has too many vertices to enumerate explicitly. Nondeterminism lets us re-verify on the fly.
  • NL = coNL is for log space, not polynomial time. The proof technique relies on configurations being log-size. It doesn't extend to NP — there's no analogous inductive counting for NP-witnesses.

4 · Q&A defence notes
  • Q1. Why does counting help?
    Once we know cn, we can verify "v is unreachable" by nondeterministically witnessing all cn reachable vertices (each different from v) and verifying each is reachable. If we account for all cn and v isn't among them, v must be unreachable.
  • Q2. Why does this work for NL but not for NP?
    In NL, configurations are polynomial in number (poly(n)) and log-size, so counting them and re-verifying each fits in log space. In NP, the witness space is exponential and the time budget is poly — there's no way to count witnesses inductively in poly time.
  • Q3. What does NL = coNL really mean?
    PATH ∈ NL means "is there a path?" can be verified by a nondeterministic log-space machine. NL = coNL means UNREACHABILITY ∈ NL — "is there NO path?" can also be verified that way. NL is "self-dual" under complement, unlike NP (where NP = coNP is conjectured false).
  • Q4. What other classes are closed under complement?
    P (deterministic class). NSPACE(f) for any f ≥ log n. coNL itself. But NP and coNP appear distinct, although unproven. The difference seems related to the time/space asymmetry — space is reusable, time isn't.
