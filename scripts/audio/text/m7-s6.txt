Practice for Module 7 lives in Sipser eight point four and nine point one. Must-do exercises: eight point thirty-four for log-space reductions; nine point five for the time hierarchy theorem on specific functions; nine point eleven for the space hierarchy theorem and its consequences. As you do them, appreciate that hierarchy theorems are the rare wins of complexity theory — proofs are constructive, results are unconditional, and they give us the only firm landmarks on the otherwise foggy P-versus-N-P map.
