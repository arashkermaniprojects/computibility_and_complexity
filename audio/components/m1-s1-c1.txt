This is the template every proof in module one will follow. To show a language L is decidable, you describe a Turing machine that on input angle-x always halts, and accepts exactly when x is in L. No looping, no maybe — a yes-no answer in finite time. The verb "describe" matters. Sipser does this at the high level — sketch the algorithm in English and argue it halts — rather than writing out transition tables. That is the convention for the rest of the module, and for the course.
