As we watch, notice how the same vertices are highlighted in both graphs. In the original graph, these vertices form a k-clique, meaning every vertex is connected to every other vertex in that set. 

Now, when we switch to the complement graph, the edges flip. So, instead of being connected, those vertices are now disconnected. This leads us to the concept of a vertex cover. A vertex cover in the complement graph means that every edge in this graph is covered by at least one of the vertices in our set. Since those vertices form a k-clique in the original graph, it naturally follows that the remaining vertices must cover the edges in the complement.
