ALLCFG is undecidable

Build a CFG GM,w that generates everything EXCEPT accepting computation histories of M.

CLO-2R1·30 ptsL7
1 · Theory

A CFG cannot check that "Ci+1 follows from Ci" because matching positions requires unbounded memory. But it CAN check the NEGATION: find one local violation.

Reduction ATM ≤ ¬ALLCFG

Build CFG GM,w that generates strings x with at least one local violation:

  • x doesn't parse as a history, OR
  • C1 isn't the start, OR
  • some (Ci, Ci+1) violates δ, OR
  • Cℓ isn't accepting.

L(GM,w) = Σ* iff no accepting history exists iff M does not accept w. So a decider for ALLCFG would decide ¬ATM.

3 · Q&A defence notes
  • Q1. Why is "find ONE violation" easier than "check ALL pairs are correct"?
    Existential vs universal. A CFG can nondeterministically pick a position and locally verify a problem there. Checking "no problems anywhere" requires global consistency, beyond CFG power.
  • Q2. Why is the trick: write Ci+1 backwards?
    In the standard construction, alternate configurations are written reversed, so a comparison between adjacent configs becomes a palindrome-like local match — within CFG capability.
  • Q3. What's EQCFG?
    {⟨G₁, G₂⟩ : L(G₁) = L(G₂)}. Undecidable as a corollary: fix G* with L(G*) = Σ*; then ALLCFG(G) iff EQCFG(G, G*).
