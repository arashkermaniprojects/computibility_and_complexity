ADFA, EDFA, ACFG are all decidable

Three poly-time deciders side by side: simulation, reachability, CYK.

CLO-1CLO-2R1·30 ptsL7
1 · Theory

Three "easy" questions about finite/context-free models, all polynomial-time decidable. Contrast with their TM cousins, which are all undecidable.

Three deciders
  • ADFA: simulate the DFA on the input. O(n).
  • EDFA: BFS from start; non-empty iff some accept state is reachable. O(|Q|).
  • ACFG: convert to CNF; run CYK dynamic programming. O(n³).

Why are these decidable while their TM versions aren't? Because DFAs and CFGs are finite-state: bounded configurations, bounded derivations.

3 · Q&A defence notes
  • Q1. Why is ADFA decidable but ATM isn't?
    DFA has |Q| configurations, all reachable in |w| steps. TM can loop indefinitely.
  • Q2. Why is EDFA O(|Q|) but ETM undecidable?
    For DFA: reachability is a simple graph problem on finitely many vertices. For TM: would require ranging over all possible inputs.
  • Q3. Where does the O(n³) for CYK come from?
    Table T[i,j] has O(n²) entries; computing each tries O(n) split points and a constant number of rules.
  • Q4. Is EQDFA decidable?
    Yes — symmetric difference of two DFAs is empty iff they're equivalent; use EDFA on the product construction.
