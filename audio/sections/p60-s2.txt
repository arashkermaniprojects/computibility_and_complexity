Let’s dive into the world of regex equivalence and how it becomes quite complex when we add squaring. Imagine you have a regular expression that can describe certain patterns in strings, and then you introduce the ability to square those patterns. This squaring means you’re essentially allowing the regex to repeat itself in a way that can create exponential growth in the number of possible strings it can match.

Now, think about how this impacts equivalence checking. When you want to know if two regex expressions are equivalent, the task becomes much harder. You’re not just checking if they match the same simple patterns anymore; you’re dealing with a potentially massive set of combinations due to the squaring. This is where things get tricky and lead us into exponential space complexity, making the problem quite challenging.
